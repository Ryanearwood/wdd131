Ordering Tips

Ordering plants and equipment for your water garden can be a daunting task, especially if you're new to water gardening. Here are some tips to help you make the right choices:

  • Know Your Zone

    Before ordering plants, make sure you know your USDA hardiness zone. This will help you choose plants that are suitable for your climate.

  • Choose the Right Equipment

    Invest in high-quality equipment. While it may be more expensive upfront, it will save you money in the long run as it will last longer and require less maintenance.

  • Consider the Size of Your Garden

    When ordering plants and equipment, consider the size of your water garden. You don't want to overcrowd your garden or choose equipment that is too large or small.

  • Ask for Help

    If you're unsure about what to order, don't hesitate to ask for help. You can reach out to us or join a water gardening community to get advice from experienced gardeners.

Ranking Top 5

Check out our top 5 picks in water gardening:

  1. Water Lily

    Known for their beautiful flowers, water lilies are a classic choice for any water garden.

  2. Koi Fish

    Koi fish are not only beautiful, but they also help keep your pond clean by eating algae.

  3. Lotus Plant

    The lotus plant is a symbol of purity and beauty, and it can be a stunning addition to your water garden.

  4. Fountain Pump

    A fountain pump is a great way to keep the water in your garden moving, which can help prevent the growth of algae.

  5. Pond Liner

    A good quality pond liner is essential for maintaining the health of your water garden.
