Learn to Create Your Own Water Garden

Welcome to our Water Gardening Learning Hub! Here, we aim to guide you on your journey to creating your very own water garden. Whether you're looking to build a simple pond or an intricate water feature, we have the resources to help you get started. We provide step-by-step guides on setting up your first water garden, from choosing the right location to selecting the best plants and fish. We also explore the different types of water gardens you can create, including ponds, fountains, and waterfalls. Join us as we dive into the world of water gardening, and start creating your own tranquil oasis today.

Getting Started

Creating a water garden begins with planning. You'll need to choose a suitable location in your backyard, taking into account factors like sunlight, soil type, and proximity to your home. Next, you'll need to decide on the type of water garden you want to create. This could be a simple pond, a bubbling fountain, or even a cascading waterfall. Each type of water garden has its own unique requirements and considerations, so it's important to do your research. Once you've planned your water garden, the next step is construction. This involves digging the pond or setting up the water feature, installing the pump and filtration system, and adding the plants and fish. Maintaining your water garden is also crucial. This includes regular cleaning, feeding the fish, and caring for the plants. With our comprehensive guides and tutorials, you'll have all the information you need to start your water gardening journey. We can't wait to see what you create!

Liner

Length of Pond + (Depth x 2) + 2 = Length of Liner
Example: 10' + 4' (average depth x 2) + 2 = 16 feet long
Width of Pond + (Depth x 2) + 2 = Width of Liner
Example: 5' + 4' (average depth x 2) + 2 = 11 feet wide

Volume

Length x Width x average Depth x 7.5 (# of gallons in a cu. ft.) = gallons of water in pond
Example: 16' x 11' x 2' x 7.5 = 2,640 gallons

Pump Sizes

Fountain: Spouting figures use 90-170 gallon per hour pump
Waterfall: For every inch of width at widest point in stream multiply by 100 to get pump size in GPH (gallons per hour). If size is not a pump size, use the next larger pump.
Example: 24 inches (widest point) x 100 = 2,400 gallon per hour pump

Filters

Should be sized to work with the pump.
Can be internal or external
Biofilter: Circulate half the volume of water in pond each hour.
Example: If you have a 700-gallon pond you need at least a 350-gph pump.
Heavier fish levels need greater filtration.

Plants

Keep the water clear, shade the pond, and provide hiding places for fish.
Shade Coverage: 60-70% of pond's surface should be covered with plants for a pond in full sun.
One water lily covers 10-15 square feet.
Underwater grasses / oxygenating plants:
A pond less than 25 square feet, one clump per square feet.
A pond more than 25 square feet, one clump per 2 square feet.

Fish

Pond less than 25 square feet, 1 inches of fish per square foot of pond.
Pond more than 25 square feet, 2 inches of fish per square foot of pond.

Snails & Tadpoles

One per square foot of surface area.
