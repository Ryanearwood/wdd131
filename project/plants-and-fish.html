Learn About Aquatic Plants and Fish

Welcome to our Water Gardening Learning Hub! Here, we aim to guide you on your journey to creating your very own water garden. In this section, we will explore the different types of aquatic plants and fish that you can include in your water garden. Aquatic plants not only add beauty to your water garden, but they also play a crucial role in maintaining the health of the ecosystem. They provide oxygen, food, and shelter for the fish and other aquatic creatures in your pond. Fish add life and color to your water garden. They also play a role in controlling pests and algae, and they contribute to the nutrient cycle of the pond. Join us as we dive into the world of water gardening, and start creating your own tranquil oasis today.

Aquatic Plants

Aquatic plants are a key component of any water garden. They add beauty and create a natural environment for the fish and other aquatic creatures. Here are some types of aquatic plants you might consider for your water garden:

Water Lilies

Water Lilies are perhaps the most popular and recognizable aquatic plants. They provide shade for the fish and help to oxygenate the water.

Cattails

Cattails are tall, reed-like plants that are great for providing cover for fish and other aquatic creatures.

Water Hawthorn

Water hawthorn is a deep water plant that produces beautiful white flowers. It’s a great choice for adding beauty to your water garden.

Waterweed Plants

Waterweed plants are fully submerged plants that help oxygenate the water and provide habitat for aquatic creatures.

Lotus

Known for their large, round leaves and stunning flowers, lotus plants can be a centerpiece in any water garden.

Fish

Fish add life and color to your water garden. They also play a role in controlling pests and algae, and they contribute to the nutrient cycle of the pond. Here are some types of fish you might consider for your water garden:

Koi

Koi are large, colorful fish that are a favorite in many water gardens. They are hardy and can live for many years with proper care.

Goldfish

Goldfish are a popular choice for water gardens. They come in a variety of colors and sizes, and they are relatively easy to care for.

Shubunkins

Shubunkins are a type of single-tailed goldfish. They are hardy and have beautiful, calico patterns.

Planting and Maintenance

Planting:

  • Select potting container.
  • Fill the container 1/2 full of soil.
  • Push in fertilizer tablets according to directions.
  • Plant bog plant in the center of the pot.
  • Cover roots with soil and tamp down.
  • Cover soil with 1/2 in. pea gravel.
  • Saturate soil with water.
  • Lower potted lily into pool to a depth of 18-24 in. of water over soil surface. If the lily has few or no leaves, place it in 6-8 in. of water for the first few weeks to encourage plant growth.
  • Place in pond at correct water depth (adjusting depth of pot as needed.) Most marginals do best in 1/2 - 3 inches of water over the top edge of the pot.

Spring/Summer Maintenance:

  • Fertilize according to directions during growing season.
  • Remove dead leaves and spent flowers.
  • Divide and repot every 1-2 years.

Fall/Winter Maintenance:

  • Stop fertilizing.
  • After frost, cut back dead leaves and stems.
  • Leave hardy marginals in place in the pond (unless it's borderline not being winter hardy to your area, in which case put it deeper). If plants are in an above ground pond/tub, remove them and place near a window in a cellar or garage. Keep cool and moist until spring.
  • Treat tropical marginals as annuals or bring them in before frost. Many tropical marginals make excellent houseplants.
